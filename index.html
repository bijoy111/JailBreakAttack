Abstract

As Large Language Models (LLMs) become increasingly integrated into diverse applications, their susceptibility to misuse for generating harmful content poses significant security risks. This paper introduces novel jailbreak attacks designed to exploit these vulnerabilities by embedding harmful prompts within seemingly benign sequences, such as game environments, dialogue completions, and question banks. Our methodology leverages automated template-based formatting to obfuscate malicious content, requiring minimal manual effort and only black-box access. We evaluate the effectiveness of these attacks on both open-source and closed-source models, demonstrating their ability to bypass existing safety mechanisms. Our findings reveal critical gaps in current LLM defenses, underscoring the need for more robust security measures. This research contributes to a deeper understanding of LLM vulnerabilities and provides a foundation for developing more resilient safeguards.
